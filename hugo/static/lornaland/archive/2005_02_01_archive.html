Sunday, February 20, 2005

johnson's party...

Busy day yesterday.

Dan woke me up at 12.00pm to remind me that Skene, Jo and James would be arriving in 4 hours! Oh my god!

I dragged myself out of bed and into the shower then immediately started preparing for their arrival. Dan and Ron were sent to the shop to buy ingredients for dinner and booze for the party. I stayed at home baking and feeling like a proper housewife!

With one guest a vegetarian and one guest a pure carnivore, the choice of what to cook for dinner was very difficult! In the end, I settled for pizzas. Everyone loves pizzas and it would suit everyone's needs.

By the time Dan and Ron got back from shopping, I had finished making the pizza dough and it was sitting on the side where it would rise for at least the next hour. I was just about to start making the onion bhaji's for the party when Dan asked if I'd made enough dough. "Of course I have, I doubled the recipe". Oh yes, I'd doubled the recipe I use to feed 2 people expecting it to feed 6 people. Arrgh! So back to making pizza dough and all that kneading!

Finally the pizza dough was made and it was time to start making the bhajis and I was still making bhajis when everyone arrived. I had at least, managed to spare 20 minutes when the beaus mixture was sitting to sort out my hair.

Dinner was a great success. Everyone seemed to enjoy making their own pizza and the choice of toppings suited everyone's need :o)

Soon it was time to go to Jansenism party which this time, was a "bring a hat" party.



We had a good night and met up with some of Johnson's friends who we hadn't seen for a while. Boys were boys and made a tower of Boddington cans which of course was knocked over on purpose by someone else. Dan and Ron started to hide bottle tops around Johnson's flat for him to find later and the obligatory spilage on the carpet was unfortunately red wine which was quickly coated in a ton of salt.

All in all it was a very good night and we were very lucky to get home alive after Mr Taxi Man's speedy driving along the A4!

And of course the evening was captured in pictures.

Sunday, February 13, 2005

day out in london...

Went to London yesterday to celebrate Mr Skene's birthday.

In order to save a wee bit of money, we decided to drive to their house. What I hadn't realised, was that this meant driving right through the centre of London. Yes, our lovely old Escort even drove down Knightsbridge, passed Harrods! :o)

We finally arrived in Surrey Quays sometime after 1.00pm. After a quick lunch (yummy toasted sandwiches) we headed off for a trip to The Science Museum.

Its fab you don't have to pay to get in to the museums now and provided us with hours of fun. I learnt that I don't know too much about energy (oops) but I did learn the best type of wheels to use on a railway! Who said education wasn't fun ;o)

When the museum chucked us out, we headed off in search of food before we made our way to The Yorkshire Grey pub, where the festivities for Skene's birthday would take place.

The next morning was Skene's birthday and Jo treated us all to a very tasty breakfast - bagels with a choice of smoked salmon & cream cheese or bacon and egg (or for the boys, one of each)

After breakfast, we said our goodbyes and headed for home. This time it was my turn to navigate which was not an easy task due to the one way systems and trying to read the directions backwards!

We finally made it home in just over an hour. The drive to and from London wasn't as bad as I thought and we'd certainly do it again.

A small selection of photos were taken to capture the day.

L x

Happy 27th Birthday Skene! You're now officially in your late 20's! :o)
