Thursday, October 26, 2006

daddy drives the train...

Had the day off yesterday to watch my Daddy be a train driver for the day at the East Somerset Railway.

I’d always said I was going to buy him a steam train driving day for his 60th and since he became a pensioner in August, that’s exactly what I did, although he had to wait until October before he got to go.

We travelled across to Shepton Mallet in Somerset on Tuesday evening to meet up with my Mam and Dad. We’d booked a room at the Burnt House Farm B&B for the night to avoid having a early morning drive.

Dad had to report for duty at 9.00am at the Engine Sheds on Wednesday morning and spent the next 3 hours getting the engine ready. There were 3 trains during the day – 12.00pm, 1.30pm and 3.00pm. Due to legal/insurance/red-tape issues, he wasn’t allowed to drive any of these trains but was on the footplate helping with the fire, signalling and coupling of the train. But between the 1.30pm and 3.00pm train, he got to be driver for me, Dan and my Mam only. I was lucky and allowed to ride on the footplate too with him so could watch him in action as he drove the train.

Dan was feeling left out but he got to ride on the footplate on the last train so he was happy too!

Sunday, October 08, 2006

weekend up north...

Back home after a weekend "at home".

My journey up was very random due to the extortionist cost of train tickets these days. It's Ok if you know you want to travel in 10 years time but if you need to get anywhere quickly, you pay a hefty price. I'd been looking at train fares from Reading and London up to Newcastle and was being quoted over £100 for a standard class return ticket. So, I was on a mission to find a cheap way of getting up north.

I was going up alone for a girls day out to celebrate Helen's 30th birthday so I started checking fares from stations around the Nottingham area as Dan was going to be spending the weekend at his Mam's. Eventually, I managed to find cheap trains from Newark - £16 up and £21.50 back. So the plan was to drive up to Newark on Friday and catch the train from there. My cheap ticket did mean that I was on the 10.30am train back down on Sunday morning!

Saturday was an all day sesh in Newcastle. We met in Lloyds Bar on the Quayside at 12.00pm and I got picked up on Saturday evening at 12.45am!

It was a great day and I managed to come home reasonably sober considering my 12.5 hour session! :o)
